Taj Mahal - jewel of love

]
The Taj Mahal, commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. Built in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as an everlasting symbol of love and devotion.
Constructed with pristine white marble and adorned with intricate carvings, inlay work of semi-precious stones, and verses from the Quran, the Taj Mahal represents the finest example of Mughal architecture. The structure is perfectly symmetrical, with a central dome surrounded by four minarets.
The Taj Mahal changes hues with the time of day ‚Äî pinkish in the morning, pearly white in the evening, and golden under moonlight. Its reflection in the Yamuna River and the lush gardens surrounding it add to its breathtaking beauty.
Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors every year and remains one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It is not just a monument, but a timeless story of love etched in stone.
