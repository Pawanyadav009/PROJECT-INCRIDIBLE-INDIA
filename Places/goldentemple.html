Golden Temple - The Spiritual Heart of India

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar, Punjab, and is the holiest shrine in Sikhism. The temple is renowned for its stunning golden architecture and its significance as a symbol of peace and unity.

Built in the 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev, the Golden Temple is surrounded by a beautiful man-made pool called the Amrit Sarovar, which is considered sacred. Pilgrims from all over the world visit the temple to seek spiritual solace and participate in the community kitchen (Langar).

The temple's gold-plated facade, peaceful surroundings, and the sound of the daily prayers create a serene atmosphere. It is a place of inclusivity, where people of all faiths are welcomed.

Tips for Visit

  • Best season: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Dress modestly and cover your head when entering the temple.
  • Visit the Golden Temple early morning to avoid crowds.
  • Make sure to take part in the Langar (community kitchen), which serves free meals to all.
  • Don't forget to walk around the sacred pool for a complete experience.
