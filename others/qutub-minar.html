Qutub Minar - Pride of Delhi

Qutub Minar, built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, is the tallest brick minaret in the world at 73 meters. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a symbol of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture, with detailed carvings and verses from the Quran. The surrounding complex includes the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar of Delhi, a marvel of ancient metallurgy.

Location: Mehrauli, Delhi

Coordinates: 28.52° N, 77.18° E

Tips for Visit

  • Best season: November–February
  • Entry fee applies
  • Guides available at the entrance
  • Evening photography is excellent
